People

Our Team

The linguists, community elders, and collaborators who have contributed to documenting and preserving the nDrapa (Zhaba) language of western Sichuan.

Research Lead

Principal Investigators

SH
Victor Junnan Pan
Principal Investigator
Professor in theoretical linguist at the Chinese University of Hong Kong.
GH
Yang Huang
Co-Investigator
Professor at Southwest Jiaotong University. Over 10 years of fieldwork in Daofu and Yajiang counties. A reference grammar on nDrapa.
PH
Peggy Mok
Co-Investigator
Professor in phonetics at the Chinese University of Hong Kong.
Researchers

Documentation Team

YW
Yang Wang
Field Linguist
PhD candidate specializing in nDrapa phonetics and tonal analysis. Has conducted extensive audio recordings with speakers across Daofu County since 2018.
LC
Li Chen
Computational Linguist
Develops the digital infrastructure for the archive, including the search interface, audio playback system, and data visualization tools.
AD
Andrew Dolan
Documentation Specialist
Linguistic anthropologist focused on nDrapa oral traditions and narrative structure. Works with elders to record and transcribe traditional tales.
DT
Dawa Tsering
Audio Engineer
Manages field recordings, audio restoration, and the digital audio archive. Ensures high-quality preservation of all spoken language data.
KZ
Kunzang Zangpo
Community Liaison
Coordinates with nDrapa community organizations across Daofu and Yajiang. Facilitates community consent, feedback, and collaborative research partnerships.
MW
Mei Wang
Archivist
Digital archivist specializing in endangered language metadata standards. Manages data organization, cataloging, and open-access distribution.
With Gratitude

Acknowledgment of Speakers & Community

This archive would not exist without the knowledge, generosity, and patience of the nDrapa elders and community members who have shared their language with us. We are deeply grateful to the late A. Phuntsok (1942–2023) and T. Wangmo (1938–2022), whose voices are preserved in these recordings and whose dedication to passing on nDrapa tradition inspires everything we do. We also thank the Daofu County Cultural Bureau, the Ganzi Tibetan Prefecture Education Bureau, and the Institute of Ethnology at Sichuan University for their partnership and guidance.
