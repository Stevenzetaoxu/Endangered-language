People

Our Team

The linguists, community elders, and collaborators who have contributed to documenting and preserving the Ainu language.

Research Lead

Principal Investigators

SK
Sayaka Kimura
Principal Linguist
Associate Professor of Linguistics at Hokkaido University. Specializes in Ainu morphosyntax and language documentation. 18 years of fieldwork in the Saru River region.
HN
Hiroshi Nakagawa
Senior Linguist (Emeritus)
Professor Emeritus at Chiba University. Author of the definitive Ainu grammar and Ainu–English dictionary. Over 40 years dedicated to Ainu linguistic research.
TM
Takeshi Murakami
Cultural Consultant
Ainu cultural practitioner and community elder from Nibutani, Biratori. Expert in traditional Ainu ceremonies, oral literature, and textile arts.
Researchers

Documentation Team

AI
Aiko Ito
Field Linguist
PhD candidate specializing in Ainu phonetics and prosody. Has conducted extensive audio recordings with speakers across Hokkaido since 2019.
RS
Ryo Sato
Computational Linguist
Develops the digital infrastructure for the archive, including the search interface, audio playback system, and data visualization tools.
EM
Emily Morris
Documentation Specialist
Linguistic anthropologist focused on Ainu oral traditions and narrative structure. Works with elders to record and transcribe yukar epics.
KS
Kenji Sato
Audio Engineer
Manages field recordings, audio restoration, and the digital audio archive. Ensures high-quality preservation of all spoken language data.
YY
Yuki Yoshida
Community Liaison
Coordinates with Ainu community organizations across Hokkaido. Facilitates community consent, feedback, and collaborative research partnerships.
MT
Maria Tanaka
Archivist
Digital archivist specializing in endangered language metadata standards. Manages data organization, cataloging, and open-access distribution.
With Gratitude

Acknowledgment of Speakers & Community

This archive would not exist without the knowledge, generosity, and patience of the Ainu elders and community members who have shared their language with us. We are deeply grateful to the late Mrs. K. Nakamura (1931–2022) and Mrs. T. Ito (1928–2024), whose voices are preserved in these recordings and whose dedication to passing on Ainu tradition inspires everything we do. We also thank the Nibutani Ainu Language School, the Ainu Cultural Resources Center in Shiraoi, and the Hokkaido Ainu Association for their ongoing partnership and guidance.
