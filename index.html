About the Language

Preserving Ainu for Future Generations

Ainu (Ainu itak) is the traditional language of the Ainu people, the indigenous inhabitants of Hokkaido, Sakhalin, and the Kuril Islands. Classified as critically endangered by UNESCO, it is currently spoken fluently by only a handful of elders, though revitalization efforts have been growing steadily.

Unlike Japanese, Ainu is an isolate language with no demonstrated genetic relationship to any other language family. It features a rich system of noun incorporation, polysynthetic verb morphology, and a complex system of verbal prefixes that mark person, number, and valence.

This archive represents over 15 years of fieldwork — thousands of hours of recordings, transcriptions, and linguistic analysis — all made freely available to researchers, community members, and anyone curious about this remarkable language.
