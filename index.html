About the Language

Preserving nDrapa for Future Generations

nDrapa (ndʐa⁵⁵ skha⁵⁵), also known as Zhaba or Zaba, is a Tibeto-Burman language of the Qiangic branch, spoken in the Xianshui River valley of western Sichuan Province, China. Classified as vulnerable by UNESCO, it is currently spoken by approximately 7,800 people, though the number is slowly declining.

nDrapa is a highly agglutinating language with a complex system of directional verb prefixes (ə- upward, a- downward, kə- inward, ŋʌ- outward) that encode both spatial orientation and aspect. It features a four-copula system, an egophoricity distinction, and a rich inventory of nine vowels with nasal contrast and four lexical tones.

This archive represents over 12 years of fieldwork — thousands of hours of recordings, transcriptions, and linguistic analysis — all made freely available to researchers, community members, and anyone curious about this remarkable language.
