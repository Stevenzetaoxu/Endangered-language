About the Language

Preserving nDrapa for Future Generations

nDrapa is a Tibeto-Burman language of the Qiangic branch, spoken in the Xianshui River valley of western Sichuan, China. Classified as vulnerable by UNESCO, it is currently spoken by approximately 7,800 people. This archive represents over 12 years of fieldwork — thousands of hours of recordings, transcriptions, and linguistic analysis.
